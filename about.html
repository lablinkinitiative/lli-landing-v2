About LabLink Initiative

A 501(c)(3) nonprofit co-founded by two people who bridged the gap from community college to competitive STEM and business careers — and built an organization to help others do the same.

Our Mission

"Educate, Empower, Connect"

LabLink Initiative educates and empowers college-aged students by providing relevant career experiences. We connect students with valuable opportunities in STEM and Business through partnerships with National Labs, federal agencies, and industry partners — bridging the gap between academic learning and professional application.

We exist because opportunity shouldn't depend on who you know. Too many qualified students — especially at community colleges — never even learn that paid STEM internship and research opportunities exist. We fix that.

All of our programs are free for students. We're funded by grants, sponsorships, and donations from people who believe access to great opportunities shouldn't have a price tag.

Leadership

Founded by people who made the journey

Michael and Sean built LabLink Initiative because they went from community college and university to working at national laboratories — and knew that most students never get the chance because no one tells them it's possible.

Michael Sarmiento

Co-Founder & Executive Director

Michael's path is the model LabLink Initiative was built to replicate. He started at community college and made it to DOE/Idaho National Laboratory, the California Environmental Protection Agency (CARB), and NNSA/Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory — three of the most competitive science and policy institutions in the country.

His background in Business Analytics gives LLI a perspective that most STEM-focused organizations miss: business and policy students belong in these spaces too. National labs need analysts, operations managers, project coordinators, and communicators — not just scientists.

As PTK (Phi Theta Kappa) President at Sacramento City College in 2024–25, Michael co-organized LLI's first major public event — a national webinar with INL that reached hundreds of community college students. He understands the community college experience from the inside, and leads LLI's partnerships, programming, and strategy with that perspective.

Sean Florez

Co-Founder & Director of Technology Innovation

Sean is a Ph.D. Materials Scientist with a research and engineering career that spans the DOE national laboratory system, the U.S. Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL), and the Department of Defense's Joint Staff as a Foreign Technology Officer. He earned his Ph.D. in Materials Science from the University of Colorado Boulder, and his B.S. from the University of Florida.

He holds two U.S. patents (US-2024-0076793A1 and US-1154-4916B2), reflecting the depth and novelty of his research contributions. His experience across DOE, DoD, and AFRL spans the full spectrum of federally-funded science — giving LLI connections and credibility with some of the most competitive programs in the country.

Sean architected and built the CDP (Career Development Platform) — LLI's AI-powered platform for skill gap assessment, personalized learning pathways, and internship matching. He leads technology strategy, platform development, and technical partnerships, and keeps LLI at the forefront of what's possible.
